Module 6

Migration as a Bias Domain

Migration bias is not just another demographic category. It combines deservingness, threat, criminality, assimilation, humanitarian need, legality, and national-origin hierarchy.

Core Readings

  • Zhu et al. (2024), nationality bias in ChatGPT.
  • CIVICS: culturally informed values in LLMs.
  • PapersPlease and Digital Gatekeepers: LLMs in immigration decision settings.
  • Hainmueller and Hopkins (2014), public attitudes toward immigration.
  • De Genova (2002), migrant illegality and deportability.
  • Menjivar and Abrego (2012), legal violence.

Key Constructs

  • Deservingness: who is worthy of protection, aid, admission, or sympathy?
  • Threat: economic, cultural, security, demographic, or status threat.
  • Legality: how legal categories become moral categories.
  • Assimilation: who is treated as integrable or culturally distant?
  • National-origin hierarchy: how country of origin shapes perceived risk or legitimacy.
