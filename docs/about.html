About This Site

This site is a shareable crash course for new students and collaborators interested in LLMs, migration, social bias, and information retrieval.

Pedagogical Assumption

The reader has moderate familiarity with machine learning, NLP, or information retrieval. The course therefore emphasizes theoretical grounding and research design rather than basic definitions of language models.

Core Position

Bias is treated as an umbrella, not the only object of study. The course connects LLM behavior to social-science concepts such as stereotype content, threat, deservingness, dehumanization, framing, legal violence, and epistemic authority.

How To Use It

Read one module at a time. Each module ends with a check question that can be used for group discussion, lab onboarding, or project scoping.
